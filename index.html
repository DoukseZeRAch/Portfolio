My foundation was architecture — a discipline I defined as une science sensible, un art sensé : a field where technical rigour and human sensitivity are not opposites but dependencies. It taught me to think in structure and form simultaneously — to organize complexity before giving it shape, to understand constraints before designing within them.

That knowledge never left me. It followed me into audiovisual production, where I learned that immersive projection mapping is not an artistic gesture — it is a precision engineering problem. Real-time data flows, hardware synchronization, spatial calibration, software systems managing gigabytes of visual content across multiple surfaces. The art was the intention. The science was the execution.

I was able to explain video production with mathematics — colour managed in 3D vector spaces, pixels organized in matrices, luminance encoded in 8-bit or 10-bit gradients. The creative decision and the technical equation were always the same thing.

When I moved into AI, the translation was natural.

Today I design the invisible architectures that make AI produce meaningful output. Prompt engineering, as I practice it, is a form of Socratic dialogue — a maieutic art guided by semantic equations that reduce the infinite probabilistic space of a language model into a precise, intentional response.

I am not an engineer who learned to be creative.
I am a creative who learned to engineer.

Intent

AI Creator · Prompt Engineer

What this role requires is someone who can think in both languages simultaneously — the language of creative intention and the language of generative systems. Someone who has stood in the edit suite making frame-by-frame decisions, and who can now encode that knowledge into prompt architectures that scale.

A 15-second commercial is not a short film. It is a constraint system. Every frame carries intent. Every cut is a decision. The opening must earn attention in under two seconds, the middle must build desire, the end must trigger action — and all of this must feel effortless to the viewer.

But it is also a technical problem. How to place a logo so it anchors without interrupting. How to respect a brand's colour system with precision, not approximation. How to calibrate music so the emotional beat lands exactly where the visual cut does. How to read a company, understand its audience, and distil that understanding into a sequence that communicates in seconds.

I have solved these problems by hand, hundreds of times. I know what makes them work — not intuitively, but analytically. A storyboard is not a drawing. It is a logical sequence encoded in visual language, carrying brand constraints, narrative structure, and audience psychology simultaneously.

My intention in this role is simple : to bring that accumulated knowledge into the architecture of your system.

Not as a consultant who observes from the outside — but as someone who sits at the intersection of creative direction and generative engineering, and builds the frameworks that make both speak the same language.

Find the common points without confusing the planes.

Avance

AI Career Guidance Platform

Career guidance for young people has long relied on generic personality tests that tell you who you are — but not what to do next. Avance was built to close that gap.

The platform guides users through a structured self-assessment, then produces a personalised career profile — matched to real occupational frameworks, translated into actionable steps, and delivered in their language.

My role was not only to build the system — it was to design the logic underneath it. How a question should be interpreted. How a response should be weighted. How personality dimensions should map to career paths.

The real challenge was translation — not linguistic, but conceptual. Taking the complexity of psychometric analysis and rendering it into responses that are coherent, warm, and genuinely meaningful to a young person navigating their future for the first time.

The prompt architecture is the product. A library of specialised prompts — each carrying a persona, a set of conditional rules, and a structured output format — working together to transform a test result into something that feels personal, not generated.

The system was co-designed with a career coach specialising in youth guidance — ensuring that the AI output was not only technically sound, but genuinely useful in a real accompaniment context.

Available in multiple languages. Deployed in production.

→ avance.today

ShieldMind

Cyberbullying Prevention Platform

Most anti-bullying programmes tell young people what to think. ShieldMind puts them inside the situation.

The platform simulates a real online conversation — with AI characters that play out the dynamics of cyberbullying in real time. Students don't observe the scenario. They participate in it, making decisions under social pressure, in the same conditions where bullying actually happens.

The design challenge was not technical — it was psychological. How to make AI characters feel credible to teenagers. How to create tension without causing harm. How to build a simulation that is immersive enough to provoke a real emotional response, and structured enough to remain safe and pedagogically sound.

My role was to translate decades of research on group psychology and adolescent behaviour into the logic of a prompt system. Each character carries a personality, a role, and a set of behavioural rules that evolve as the session unfolds. The conversation is never scripted — it is generated, in real time, from that architecture.

The result is an experience that changes how young people respond — not by telling them what to do, but by letting them discover it themselves.

The simulation was co-designed with a Rome-based team specialising in cyberbullying prevention through role-play — their fieldwork directly informed the behavioural architecture and the pedagogical structure of each scenario.

Tested in schools across France and Italy. Available in multiple languages.

→ shieldmind.eu

Hidden Team

Performance Diagnostic · B2B HR Analytics

Every team has a surface story — the one that appears in performance reviews and management reports. And then there is what actually drives it : the unspoken dynamics, the invisible tensions, the patterns that no survey has ever been designed to catch.

The Hidden Team Performance Diagnostic was built to surface the second story.

The platform collects anonymous qualitative and quantitative input from team members, then runs it through a system of specialised AI analyzers — each one anchored in a recognised framework of organisational psychology. The output is not a report. It is a structured diagnostic that gives leaders something rare : an evidence-based picture of what is actually happening inside their team.

My role began with analysis. I studied existing consulting practice in this domain — how teams were assessed, where the gaps were, what the frameworks said versus what practitioners actually needed. From that analysis, I designed the system : the questions, the analytical logic, the prompt architecture that translates human responses into strategic insight.

Each analyzer was built around a specific dimension of team health. Each prompt was engineered to produce output that is precise, actionable, and calibrated to the language of leadership.

The diagnostic framework was co-designed with a specialist in team performance and organisational development — whose expertise shaped both the questions asked and the analytical logic applied to the responses.

Deployed in production for a corporate client.
